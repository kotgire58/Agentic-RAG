# Technical Analysis: Price Action, Indicators, and Market Psychology

**Intermediate Finance Series | Technical Analysis**

Technical analysis is the study of historical price and volume data to forecast future price movements. Critics dismiss it as astrology; practitioners argue it works because enough participants act on the same signals, making them self-fulfilling. The truth lies between — technical analysis is most useful as a framework for understanding market psychology and identifying high-probability risk/reward setups.

## Price Action Fundamentals

### Candlestick Charts
Each candle represents a time period (1min, 5min, daily, weekly) with four data points:
- **Open:** First trade of the period
- **High:** Highest trade of the period
- **Low:** Lowest trade of the period
- **Close:** Last trade of the period

**Body:** Range between open and close. Green/white = close above open (bullish). Red/black = close below open (bearish).
**Wicks/shadows:** Lines extending beyond body to high/low. Long wicks show rejection of prices.

### Key Candlestick Patterns

**Doji:** Open ≈ Close. Body is tiny/nonexistent. Represents indecision — neither bulls nor bears in control. Significant after a sustained trend (potential reversal signal).

**Hammer/Hanging Man:** Small body at top of candle, long lower wick. At bottom of downtrend (hammer) = bullish reversal. At top of uptrend (hanging man) = bearish reversal. The long wick shows sellers pushed price down but buyers reclaimed the level.

**Engulfing patterns:** Bullish engulfing — large green candle completely engulfs prior red candle. Bearish engulfing — reverse. Strong reversal signals, especially at key levels with volume confirmation.

**Shooting star:** Small body at bottom, long upper wick. At resistance levels, shows buyers failed to hold higher prices. Bearish signal.

**Morning/Evening Star:** Three-candle reversal patterns. More reliable than single candles.

### Support and Resistance

**Support:** Price level where buying interest historically absorbs selling pressure, causing price to bounce. Previous highs that broke become support (polarity principle). The more times a level holds, the more significant it is — and the more explosive the break when it fails.

**Resistance:** Price level where selling pressure historically overwhelms buying. Becomes support once broken (same polarity principle).

**Dynamic support/resistance:** Moving averages, trendlines, and VWAP act as moving levels that price interacts with.

**Why levels work:** Traders cluster orders around obvious levels. Stop losses pile up just below support (triggering stop runs). Limit orders cluster at resistance. This order flow concentration creates predictable behavior.

## Trend Analysis

### Defining Trends
**Uptrend:** Series of higher highs (HH) and higher lows (HL). As long as this structure holds, trend is intact.
**Downtrend:** Series of lower highs (LH) and lower lows (LL).
**Sideways/range:** Price oscillating between support and resistance without clear directional bias.

**Trend identification rule:** In an uptrend, buy pullbacks to prior swing highs (now support). Trend reversal signal: price fails to make a new high AND breaks below prior swing low.

### Trendlines
Draw connecting swing lows in uptrend, swing highs in downtrend. Require at least 2 touches to draw, gain significance with 3+ touches.

**Channel:** Parallel trendlines containing price action. Upper channel = target for longs from lower channel.

### Moving Averages
Smooth price action to identify trend direction:

**Simple Moving Average (SMA):** Average of closing prices over N periods. 50-day and 200-day SMA most watched by institutional traders.

**Exponential Moving Average (EMA):** Weights recent prices more heavily. More responsive to recent price changes. 9-EMA and 21-EMA popular for short-term traders; 8/21 EMA crossover system widely used.

**Death Cross/Golden Cross:**
- Golden Cross: 50-day SMA crosses above 200-day SMA → Bullish long-term signal
- Death Cross: 50-day SMA crosses below 200-day SMA → Bearish long-term signal
These are lagging signals — often useful for confirming trend, not predicting turns.

**VWAP (Volume-Weighted Average Price):**
- Average price weighted by volume, reset daily
- Most important intraday reference level
- Institutional algorithms execute around VWAP
- Price above VWAP = bullish intraday bias; below = bearish
- First VWAP test often holds; second test more likely to fail

## Key Technical Indicators

### RSI (Relative Strength Index)
Oscillator measuring momentum, scaled 0-100:
- Above 70: "Overbought" — not a sell signal alone, but warns of stretched conditions
- Below 30: "Oversold" — not a buy signal alone, strong downtrends can stay oversold
- **Divergence:** Price makes new high but RSI makes lower high = bearish divergence (momentum weakening). Powerful reversal signal when combined with resistance.
- **Most useful application:** In strong trends, RSI oscillating between 40-80 (bull market range) or 20-60 (bear market range).

### MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence)
Shows relationship between two EMAs (typically 12 and 26 period):
- **MACD line:** 12 EMA - 26 EMA
- **Signal line:** 9 EMA of MACD line
- **Histogram:** MACD - Signal line (shows momentum)
- **Crossover:** MACD crossing above signal = bullish; below = bearish
- **Zero line cross:** MACD crossing above zero = uptrend confirmation
- **Best use:** Trend confirmation, not standalone timing signal

### Bollinger Bands
Three lines: 20-period SMA ± 2 standard deviations:
- **Band squeeze:** Bands contracting = low volatility → volatility expansion coming (direction unknown)
- **Band expansion:** Trending strongly; price riding outer band is continuation signal
- **Mean reversion:** Price touching upper band in range-bound conditions = overextended; expect reversion to middle band
- **BB %B:** Shows where price is within bands (above 1 = above upper band, below 0 = below lower band)

### Volume Analysis
Price moves with volume confirmation are more significant than those without:
- **Volume spike on breakout:** Institutional participation confirms the move
- **Low volume breakout:** Often fails — "false breakout" or "fakeout"
- **Volume dry-up:** At support, declining volume on pullback suggests selling exhaustion
- **On-Balance Volume (OBV):** Cumulative volume indicator; divergence from price suggests accumulation/distribution

### ATR (Average True Range)
Measures average price range over N periods (typically 14):
- Not directional — purely measures volatility
- **Stop placement:** 1.5-2× ATR stop gives position room to breathe without excessive risk
- **Position sizing:** Normalize risk across different stocks by sizing positions to equal dollar ATR risk
- **ATR expansion:** Increasing ATR = accelerating trend or volatility event; tighten stops

## Chart Patterns

### Continuation Patterns (Trend likely continues)

**Flag/Pennant:** Sharp move (flagpole) followed by brief consolidation (flag). Breakout direction usually same as initial move. Measured target = flagpole length added to breakout point.

**Cup and Handle:** Rounded bottom (cup) followed by brief pullback (handle), then breakout. William O'Neil pattern. Often precedes significant upside moves.

**Ascending Triangle:** Flat resistance with rising support. Coiling price action with upward pressure. Breakout above flat resistance often explosive.

**Descending Triangle:** Flat support with declining resistance. Bearish coiling. Breakdown below flat support often sharp.

### Reversal Patterns

**Head and Shoulders (H&S):** Left shoulder, higher head, lower right shoulder, with neckline connecting the lows. Break of neckline = trend reversal. Measured target = head to neckline distance, projected down from neckline break.

**Double Top/Bottom:** Price tests a level twice, fails to break through (or hold), and reverses. More reliable when second test comes on lower volume (less conviction).

**Rounding Bottom (Saucer):** Gradual, rounded transition from downtrend to uptrend. Long basing period followed by breakout. Tends to precede sustained moves.

## Volume Profile and Market Profile

### Volume Profile
Shows volume traded at each price level over a period:
- **High Volume Node (HVN):** Price level with heavy volume = strong support/resistance, price attracted to it
- **Low Volume Node (LVN):** Price level with minimal volume = fast movement through this area, little support
- **Point of Control (POC):** Single price level with most volume traded = strongest magnet

### Value Area
70% of volume traded within the Value Area:
- **VAH (Value Area High):** Top of value area = resistance
- **VAL (Value Area Low):** Bottom of value area = support
- **80% rule:** If price opens outside value area and enters it, 80% of the time it will reach the opposite end of value area

## Putting It Together: A Framework for Trade Setups

High-probability setups share common characteristics:
1. **Trend alignment:** Trade in direction of higher timeframe trend
2. **Level confluence:** Multiple support/resistance types agree (MA + prior high + volume node)
3. **Entry trigger:** Lower timeframe pattern at higher timeframe level
4. **Volume confirmation:** Breakout/breakdown accompanied by above-average volume
5. **Defined risk:** Clear stop level beyond which trade thesis is wrong

**The biggest mistake:** Treating technical analysis as predictive rather than probabilistic. Any setup can fail. The goal is setups where reward/risk is 2:1 or better and probability is above 50% — positive expected value over many trades, not picking winners.
